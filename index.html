Writing

My writing interests range from fun comedy projects to super serious political discussions. Relevant to that, my political and philosophical interests are absurdism, accelerationism, anarcho-syndicalism, direct action/praxis, prefigurativism, technogaianism, technoprogressivism, and transhumanism.

Developing

Unlike my writing, the programming and software development I do is a lot more closely related to my actual studies. I taught myself programming through web development in high school, and went on to apply to college as a computer science major. Due to certain issues with the program I was able to get into, this was eventually downgraded to a minor, with my major being changed to a double major in Physics and Mathematics.

For the most part, my original focus on web development has held out over time. A lot of my works in physics and math, and even regular programming, have been either solely created for or adapted for the web.

Designing

While it's never been something I've focused on, I have also done some graphic design. This, of course, varies a bit, encompassing page layouts to logo and branding.

"ORCA" was the proposed name for the high power rocket team at UWRF's physics department for the 2015-2016 Midwest High-Power Rocket Competition. The name and design were never officially adopted. They were, however, used on the grant proposal for the project. It is possible that the branding may be used in the future, as it is not necessarily unique to rocketry and the original project for which it was designed is an annual one.

The Student Voice was mentioned earlier in the writing section because I had written columns for them. While I was working there (from 2015-2016), I was mainly tasked with editing the front page. This entailed reading through the articles meant to be placed on the first or third pages, checking for any errors that had been missed, and placing them on the pages along with photos or pullquotes to make them fit the pages perfectly.

Near the end of fall 2015, the editorial team began discussing the creation of a logo for the paper, citing that there was no consistent branding between the digital and physical media that they produced. I proposed a minimalist falcon design that was included in the header of the paper for the duration of the spring 2016 semester. This was never implemented on the website and, for reasons never announced, was removed from the physical paper's header by fall 2016.
