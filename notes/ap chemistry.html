§3.8 formula mass

the formula mass/molecular mass is $$\sum_{k=0}^n N_k*m_k$$ where $n$ is the number of unique elements, $N_k$ is the number of element $k$ in the molecule, n $m_k$ is the atomic mass of element $k$. the molecular mass is numerically equivalent to the moral mass except the latter is in g/mol rather than amu. to get the ampount of molecules in a sample just divide the mass by the molar mass then multiple by Avogadro's number.

§3.9 composition

$$P = \frac{m_x}{m_{mol}} \times 100%$$ where $P$ is the percentage of the mass of a compound contributed by element $x$, $m_x$ is the mass of element $x$ in 1mol of the compound, n $m_{mol}$ is the mass of 1mol of the compound. if you're really cleaver this can get you the molecular formula... by measuring chemicals put in and their molar ratios. you can use empirical formula molar mass to make a ratio and from there get a full thing: $$F_{molecular} = \frac{m_{mol}}{m_{mol_{emp}}} \times F_{empirical}$$ where $F_{molecular}$ is the molecular formula, $m_{mol}$ is the molar mass, $m_{mol_{emp}}$ is the empirical formula molar mass, n $F_{empirical}$ is the empricial formula.
