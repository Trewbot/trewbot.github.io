§2.2 Limits Including Infinity

Horizontal Asymptotes (H.A.s) are defined by the limit of the function $f(x)$ as $x\to\infty$, and yes, the curve can cross its asymptotes (as opposed to what is often taught in pre-calculus courses). We define this asymptote as $$y=b\text{ where }\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}f(x)=b.\tag 1\\$$ Similarly, a Vertical Asymptote (V.A.) is defined as $$x=a\text{ where }\lim_{x\to\pm a}f(x)=\infty.\tag 2\\$$ A removable discontinuity is a discontinuity at a single point that can be removed by canceling terms of the function. An example of this: $\require{cancel}(2x-3)\cancel{(2x+3)}/\cancel{(2x+3)}(x-1)$.
